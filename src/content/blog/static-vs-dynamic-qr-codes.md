When researching QR codes, you will inevitably run into two terms: **Static QR Codes** and **Dynamic QR Codes**. Choosing the wrong one can lead to expensive reprints or broken links in your marketing campaigns. 

In this article, we explain the mechanics of both systems so you can make an informed, budget-friendly decision.

---

## The Technical Differences At A Glance

Here is a summary of how both formats function under the hood:

| Feature | Static QR Codes | Dynamic QR Codes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Data Encoding** | Encoded directly into the pixels | Encoded as a short redirection URL |
| **Editable Content** | No (Forever locked once generated) | Yes (Can redirect to new URLs anytime) |
| **Scan Tracking** | No (100% private) | Yes (Tracks location, device, time) |
| **Lifespan** | Permanent (Never expires) | Depends on hosting service subscription |
| **Watermark/Fees** | 100% Free (No account or fees required) | Monthly subscription fees usually apply |
| **Grid Complexity** | Increases as you add more text/data | Stays simple and easy to scan |

## What is a Dynamic QR Code?

A **Dynamic QR Code** does not contain your final destination. Instead, it contains a short link belonging to a redirection service (e.g., `https://qrs.ly/ab32x`). 
When a user scans the code:
1. The smartphone opens the redirect service link.
2. The server registers the scan metadata (IP address, location, timestamp).
3. The server redirects the user's browser to the actual destination URL.

Because the final link lives on a central server, the system owner can log into a dashboard and edit the destination URL at any point.

### The Main Risks of Dynamic QR Codes:
- **Dependence:** If the dynamic provider goes out of business, changes their pricing, or experiences downtime, your printed codes will instantly break.
- **Privacy Intrusion:** Every scan is tracked, which may violate user privacy standards.
